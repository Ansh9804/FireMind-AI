# FERL IIT Gandhinagar FAQs

**1. Do you conduct tests as per ISO standards?**
Yes, we conduct tests as per applicable ISO, ASTME, and other relevant standards depending on the testing requirement.

**2. How long is the report valid?**
The test report is applicable to the tested sample/product.

**3. Can we visit the laboratory?**
Yes, laboratory visits can be arranged with prior appointment and approval.

**4. Do you test according to NABL/BIS standards?**
We conduct testing as per applicable standards. FERL is NABL accredited under ISO/IEC 17025:2017 and recognized by BIS under the relevant scheme.

**5. Would it be okay if there is a change in the sample size or in what I want to test?**
Any change in sample size or testing requirement should be informed in advance. It will be reviewed based on the applicable standard and testing scope.

**6. What payment modes are accepted?**
Payment can be made through Bank Transfer / NEFT / RTGS. Payment details are shared in the quotation/invoice.

**7. What is the booking process?**
After receiving your requirement, we share the quotation. Once payment confirmation and required details are received, the test slot is scheduled.

**8. What documents should be sent with the sample?**
Sample details, Technical specifications, company details, and contact information.

**9. Is witness testing possible?**
Yes, it is possible to be a witness to the testing.

**10. Can the client observe the test?**
Yes, the client is observing the test.

**11. When will the test report be issued?**
The report is issued 14-15 Working days.

**12. Can we get a hard copy of the report?**
Yes, a signed hard copy can be provided or dispatched if required.

**13. Is the report NABL accredited?**
Applicable reports under the accredited scope can be issued as NABL accredited reports. Kindly share the specific test requirement.

The test slot depends on current schedule availability, sample readiness, and payment confirmation. After receiving payment, we will check and confirm the earliest available slot.
